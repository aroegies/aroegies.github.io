About

I’m a Research Scientist at Kira Systems based out of Toronto, Canada. Prior to Kira, I completed my PhD at the University of Waterloo under the supervision of Gordon V. Cormack (and sometimes Charlie Clarke, when Gordon was on sabbatical).

My primary interests are in the design and evaluation of human-in-the-loop (i.e., explicit feedback) Information Retrieval systems. This has generally been expressed through research in electronic discovery in civil litgation and due diligence law. However, when the opportunity arises I do not shy away from real-time, web-based information system research.

While at the University of Waterloo, my research was oriented towards high-recall retrieval system design and evaluation. As part of that I co-ordinated the TREC Total Recall track for both years it. In addition, I also conducted research into Twitter retireval, primarily evaluation, and was part of the TREC Real-Time Summarization track for the 2017 iteration. With Charles Clarke, Gordon Cormack, and Jimmy Lin, I explored the idea of “Searching from Mars” and the practical implications that such search systems would face.

At Kira Systems, I primarily work on developing the core machine learning technology that powers our software. My time at Kira has also allowed me to further explore my interests in Human-Computer Interaction through clsoe collaboration with our Design and Legal Knowledge Engineerign teams.
